Expertise

What I work with

A broad foundation built over years of genuine curiosity and hands-on work.

Artificial Intelligence (AI)

Not the hype — the practical side. I'm drawn to moments where AI genuinely saves someone time or makes something hard feel easy. Whether it's automating a tedious workflow or helping a team make better decisions, the goal is always real, tangible value.

Audio Visual (AV)

There's something deeply satisfying about a room that just works — where the technology disappears and the people in it can focus on collaboration. From boardrooms and council chambers to multi-screen AV installations, I enjoy building spaces that feel effortless.

Digital Infrastructure

The invisible stuff that everything else runs on. Servers, cloud platforms, storage, connected systems — I like understanding what's happening underneath, and making sure it's reliable, scalable, and well thought-through.

Information Technology (IT)

Day-to-day IT is where you learn the most about what people actually need from their tools. System setup, deployments, day-to-day operations — I find this kind of work genuinely rewarding. Keeping things running well is underrated.

Networking

Networks are like plumbing — nobody thinks about them until something breaks. I enjoy thinking about them before that happens: designing for reliability, building for performance, and making sure connectivity is something people can depend on.

Software & Apps

Building things that fit how people actually think and work is endlessly interesting. The interface is never just cosmetic — it shapes the entire experience. I care about both sides: the code that runs underneath, and the clarity of what the user sees.

Tech Support

Good support is about patience, clarity, and helping someone feel like their problem is solvable. It almost always is. I genuinely enjoy the human side of this work — being the person who makes the frustrating thing feel manageable.
